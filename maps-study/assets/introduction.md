# Can you use choropleth maps to interpret spatial patterns?

A **Choropleth Map** (shown above) uses color to represent event rates associated with geographical regions (e.g, counties or states). For example, a county with 80 out of 100 people who consume a product (X) is shaded as dark red (A), while a county with 30 out of 100 people who consume a product (X) is shaded as light red (B). 

We can visually identify numerous patterns using choropleth maps, such as regions with high consumption of product (X) (shaded as dark red) as well as regions with low consumption of product (X) (shaded as light red). 

In this study, you will be asked to use these maps to answer questions about how you interpret and make sense of patterns shown.
